Task Response

Band 7.0 — Addresses all parts of the task, clear position throughout
▼
✓
Both sides discussed clearly. Advantages (personal growth, cultural awareness, career exploration) and disadvantages (academic disruption, financial temptation) are addressed with supporting details.
✓
Introduction effectively sets up the essay. The opening paragraph provides context and explains what a gap year entails — travel, volunteering, working — establishing a clear foundation.
!
Conclusion is too generic. "Whether to take a year-long break or not is an individual decision" doesn't add analytical value. A Band 8+ essay takes a clear stance or offers a nuanced final thought beyond restating the obvious.

Grammar Enhancement — Before → After

Enhanced (Participle Clause) Travelling away from home, students develop a heightened sense of independence and self-reliance.
Original The benefits of taking a year off are plentiful.
Enhanced (Cleft Sentence) What makes a gap year appealing is the sheer breadth of benefits it offers.
Original A year is a long time and students could lose the good study habits.

¶1 — Introduction (4 sentences)
Function: Context → Definition → Examples → Purpose. Well-structured
Note: Good paraphrasing of the topic. However, no explicit thesis/position statement — the reader doesn't know YOUR stance yet. Add: "While there are clear benefits, the risks should not be underestimated."
¶2 — Advantages (5 sentences)
Function: Topic sentence → 4 parallel supporting points → Wrap-up. Excellent
Note: The four-level framework (personal → cultural → professional → intellectual) is the strongest part of this essay. To push higher, add ONE concrete example within any of the four points.
¶3 — Disadvantages (5 sentences)
Function: Topic sentence → Academic risk → Financial trap → Lost opportunity. Needs tightening
Note: The "deluded into thinking they're making a lot of money" point is interesting but underdeveloped. WHY is short-term income deceptive? Connect it to opportunity cost or lifetime earnings gap with a statistic or example.
¶4 — Conclusion (3 sentences)
Function: Restate neutrality → Advice → Life philosophy. Too safe
Note: "Time is a precious resource" feels like a proverb tacked on. A strong conclusion should: (1) briefly summarize both sides, (2) state your weighted opinion, (3) end with a forward-looking insight. Don't introduce new ideas, but don't just restate either.

Suggested Band 8+ Structure

Introduction (2–3 sentences): Paraphrase topic → Acknowledge both sides exist → State your lean: "While a gap year carries inherent risks, the developmental advantages — when pursued with intention — outweigh the drawbacks."

Teacher's Comment

This is a solid Band 7 essay that demonstrates genuine writing ability. Your paragraph organisation is clear, your ideas are logical, and you've shown awareness of both sides of the argument. The four-part parallel structure in Body 1 is particularly impressive — it's the kind of technique that catches an examiner's eye.

To reach Band 8, focus on three things: (1) Vary your vocabulary — especially replacing "students" with synonyms throughout. (2) Add complex grammar structures — practice inversions, cleft sentences, and participle clauses until they feel natural. (3) Strengthen your conclusion — take a clear position instead of sitting on the fence.

You're closer to Band 8 than you think. The foundation is strong — now it's about polishing the details.
